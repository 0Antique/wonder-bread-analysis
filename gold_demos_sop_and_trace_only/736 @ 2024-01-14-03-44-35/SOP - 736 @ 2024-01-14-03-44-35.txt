Make the LICENSE of gimmiethat.space and dotfiles to MIT license.
1. Click into the search bar at the top left of the page, with a purple background.
2. Type in the name of the first repo that the user wants to update.
3. Press enter.
4. Click on the most relevant search result, i.e. the desired repo which we can edit. We can edit a repo if we are, for example, its owner or a developer, which we can know based on the “Owner” or “Developer” tag appearing to the right of the repo’s title.
5. This project already has the MIT license, so we don’t need to change anything.
6. Click into the search bar at the top left of the page, with a purple background.
7. Type in the name of the next repo that the user wants to update.
8. Press the down arrow key to search all of the site, as opposed to within the project you are currently located in.
9. Press enter.
10. Click on the most relevant search result, i.e. the desired repo which we can edit. We can edit a repo if we are, for example, its owner or a developer, which we can know based on the “Owner” or “Developer” tag appearing to the right of the repo’s title.
11. Click on the “Add LICENSE” button, located above the table of repo contents.
12. Click on the “Apply a template” button at the top of the code editor box.
13. Type in “MIT”, the type of the desired license.
14. Press enter.
15. Scroll to the bottom of the page.
16. Click the blue button “Commit changes” near the bottom of the page.
